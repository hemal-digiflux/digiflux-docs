Definition of Terms

Owner / Accountable

The individual who is ultimately answerable for the correct and thorough completion of the task. They hold final responsibility and sign-off.

Responsible

The person or people who do the work to complete the task. There is at least one Responsible role, though others can be delegated to for assistance.

Consulted

Individuals who provide input, suggestions, or expertise. This is a two-way communication.

Informed

People who are kept up-to-date on progress but are not directly involved in the work. This is typically a one-way communication.

Key QA Authority: Blocking a Release

QA holds the authority to block a release if critical or blocker bugs are found that violate acceptance criteria. This decision must be communicated immediately with a clear impact analysis. The PM is accountable for the final go/no-go decision after consulting with QA and TCO.

Real-time Incident Examples

Scenario 1: Production Bug

Issue: Payment fails in production.
Response: PM manages stakeholders, TCO leads triage & fix, DevOps supports logs/rollback, QA validates hotfix.

Scenario 2: Deployment Failure

Issue: CI/CD pipeline fails.
Response: DevOps owns fix, TCO checks code impact, PM decides go/no-go, informs stakeholders.

Scenario 3: Data Breach Alert

Issue: Suspicious access detected.
Response: DevOps isolates systems & rotates secrets, TCO patches vulnerability, PM coordinates legal/comms, QA regression-tests auth.

Scenario 4: Third-Party Outage

Issue: Payment gateway down.
Response: TCO switches to fallback, DevOps updates env & feature flags, PM manages comms, QA runs smoke on fallback.

Scenario 5: Performance Degradation

Issue: High p95 latency.
Response: DevOps scales infra & adds profiling, TCO optimizes hotspots, QA validates perf benchmarks, PM adjusts SLAs.

Scenario 6: Hotfix Rollback

Issue: Hotfix introduces new bug.
Response: DevOps triggers rollback, TCO cherry-picks safe fix, QA executes targeted regression, PM updates customers.

Scenario 7: Compliance Deadline

Issue: Mandatory update due (e.g., RBI/PCI).
Response: PM reprioritizes scope, TCO leads technical changes, DevOps updates policies/scanners, QA signs off controls.

Scenario 8: Data Loss Risk

Issue: Backup job failing silently.
Response: DevOps fixes job & restores sample, TCO adds app-level idempotency, QA verifies DR playbook, PM audits RTO/RPO.

Scenario 9: Analytics Mismatch

Issue: Revenue numbers differ across dashboards.
Response: TCO reconciles tracking schema, DevOps checks ETL schedule, QA validates events, PM communicates corrected KPIs.
